# Outdoors, Sports, & Hobbies

Connecting with the physical world is a core tenet of Hîsyêô life, honoring [Dimîtu](../religion/gods/dimîtu) (Nature) and [Îfêstôs](../religion/gods/îfêstôs) (Effort).

## Outdoor Activities

Most outdoor activities involve the verb tuluti (to traverse/travel) or kelo (to play).

| English  | Hîsyêô                                   | Literal Meaning     |
|----------|------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Hiking   | <HisyeoText>tuluti bi wowoê</HisyeoText> | Traversing by foot  |
| Camping  | <HisyeoWord is='mûhoyon'/>               | Camp / Bivouac      |
| Swimming | <HisyeoText>tuluti xîmu</HisyeoText>     | Traversing by water |
| Running  | <HisyeoText>nô fîkî</HisyeoText>         | Fast moving         |
| Fishing  | <HisyeoText>wêtodû mîcîn</HisyeoText>    | Fish hunting        |

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>nûs kûu li nô mût tohilôk bûmî til xe tuluti bi wowoê.&emsp;<em>Let's go to the mountains to hike.</em></p>
    <p>tîton onô li kîldo êto.&emsp;<em>The path is very long.</em></p>
    <p>nûs ke tuluti lon wokût côt.&emsp;<em>We will travel for a long time.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

:::caution English "long" - Time vs. Space

Remember that Hîsyêô culture does not perceive time as a line like many european cultures do, instead time radiates out from the center like rings of a tree trunk. For describing a long path, you can safely use the word for "long" <HisyeoWord is='kîldo'/>. For describing a long time, you should not use the word for "long" because, in Hîsyêô, the appropriate adjective would be "thick" <HisyeoWord is='côt'/>.

:::

## Sports

Sports are generally referred to as <HisyeoWord is='kelo'/> (play/game). Team sports emphasize <HisyeoWord is='zomo'/> (group/team).

| English       | Hîsyêô                                 | Literal Meaning  |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|------------------|
| Sport / Game  | <HisyeoWord is='kelo'/>                | to play a game   |
| Ball          | <HisyeoWord is='gundolê'/>             | Sphere / Ball    |
| Team          | <HisyeoText>zomo kon kelo</HisyeoText> | Playing group    |
| Player        | <HisyeoText>ônî kelo</HisyeoText>      | The playing one  |
| Score / Point | <HisyeoText>wîlûwo xokôn</HisyeoText>  | Unit of value    |
| To Win        | <HisyeoText>xe li bîlûs</HisyeoText>   | To be victorious |
| To Lose       | <HisyeoText>xe li funsût</HisyeoText>  | To be defeated   |

### Specific Sports

Most sports are named by the primary object or action.

- **Soccer:** <HisyeoText>kelo wowoê</HisyeoText> (Foot game)
- **Football:** <HisyeoText>kelo yê gundolê hûwu</HisyeoText> (Game of egg ball)
- **Basketball:** <HisyeoText>kelo yê gundolê lêfôtol</HisyeoText> (Game of bouncing ball)
- **Baseball:** <HisyeoText>kelo yê nûyo kon xobo</HisyeoText> (Game of striking sticks)
- **Tennis:** <HisyeoText>kelo yê nec cîes kon xobo</HisyeoText> (Game of tight striking nets)
- **Bowling:** <HisyeoText>kelo yê gundolê tulkolyun</HisyeoText> (Game of rolling ball)

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>zomo nûs li sî bîlûs hôn kelo onô foxo.&emsp;<em>Our team won the game!</em></p>
    <p>¿wîlûwo xokôn li zik?&emsp;<em>What is the score?</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Hobbies & Skills

Hobbies are often described as <HisyeoWord is='bêûdo'/> (study) or <HisyeoWord is='tûu'/> (doing) for pleasure.

| English   | Hîsyêô                              | Literal Meaning    |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Gardening | <HisyeoText>lîun comen</HisyeoText> | Garden nurturing   |
| Painting  | <HisyeoWord is='toswîl'/>           | Painting / Drawing |
| Writing   | <HisyeoWord is='cukto'/>            | Writing            |
| Reading   | <HisyeoWord is='golo'/>             | Reading            |
| Cooking   | <HisyeoWord is='côûfon'/>           | Cooking            |
| Sewing    | <HisyeoWord is='sêmoi'/>            | Stitching / Sewing |

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo sûko xe toswîl hoî wokût fôlnî noyo.&emsp;<em>I like to paint in my free time.</em></p>
    <p>¿nîmû li ônî côûfon xôn ko?&emsp;<em>Are you a good cook?</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
