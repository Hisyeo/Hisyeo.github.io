# Home & Country

How to say where you are from and where you live now.

## Home

### Point it out

Sometimes you just need to demonstrate which place is yours.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>ulyô li niwos noyo hoî ongôlô onô.&emsp;<em>That's my house on the corner.</em></p>
    <p>noyo niwos sokofû yê cên eswen uklo.&emsp;<em>I live upstairs.</em></p>
    <p>nîo niwos sokofû yê sêl eswen en bûmî yê boxo ulyô.&emsp;<em>They live in the basement of that place.</em></p>
    <p>nûs niwos hoî ôfo.&emsp;<em>We live here.</em></p>
    <p>nîmû niwos yôk hoî ôfo.&emsp;<em>You don't live there.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

:::tip Grammar Used

The first sentence translates the "on the corner" preposition by adding it to
the predicate instead of directly connecting it to the noun phrase but that's
equivalent here because the predicate is a simple copulative sentence linking a
demonstrative to the noun complement. The second and third sentences make use
of <HisyeoWord is='sokofû'/> to mean "story" or "level" of a building. You could
also use <HisyeoWord is='sûsôn'/> but with the adjective of "higher", it should
be enough to say "higher/lower floor". The fourth and fifth sentences don't
provide a direct object to the verb even though it seems like the English
sentence has "here"/"there" in the direct object slot. This is because these
location deictic words aren't directly translatable in Hîsyêô. Instead, you
supply the demonstrative word to the location preposition <HisyeoWord is='hoî'/>.
Without a direct object, predicates rely on any added prepositions for thematic
content.

:::

:::warning Endonyms

An endonym is how a city, region, or state refers to themselves where an exonym
is how a city, region, or state is referred to by outsiders. Hîsyêô suggests
that you always try to use endonyms and that's what will be provided in the
documentation. If you are struggling to be understood because perhaps there are
multiple regional endonyms or you're speaking to a non-local person, you can try
to offer a different endonym, or as a last resolt an exonym, to your
interlocutor to see if that helps. One of the central tenets of the language is
a respect and admiration for the myriad of languages in the world and the
phonology of this language tries to be a bit wider to help illustrate this
respect. Names are another place where respect should be given.

:::

### Regions

Sometimes the name of the place where we live is not well known, in that case,
we can zoom out and talk about the region that it is located in.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo niwos sinsî cûtî hoî fenûo fîtîkôs sôltûstik yê kûôcyo Kenudu.&emsp;<em>I live in a small town in the northwest region of Canada.</em></p>
    <p>nîmû monodo sî sinsî Tuhêmu ko. ni (li gô) hoî fenûo sêl Seklumentôû sôltûstik.&emsp;<em>Have you not heard of Tehama? It's in the North Sacramento Valley.</em></p>
    <p>môi nûs bi konbokbo yoûtî niwos ûnhilun yê fenûo sinsî Lozil Bouk yê sinsî Xikogô.&emsp;<em>We mostly all live around the Rogers Park neighborhood of Chicago.</em></p>
    <p>nîmû sûko xe tûu nô sêl lûcik hoî fenûo tôxûtsi êto Olbî ko.&emsp;<em>Do you like to go skiing in the Alps?</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Country

Modern society has been designed by various forces to have us believing in
[imagined communities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imagined_community) that we
call countries, states, or nations. There are various mores and folkways that we
follow to help rebuff their importance in our lives. In some ways, this is
similar to the approach that the Hîsyêô community follows to help build a stable
home within the zeitgeist.

:::warning Sanctioned Violence

One of the values of the Hîsyêô community is respect for all humans. Some nation
states allow for violence in certain circumstances and this is considered
regressive behavior to the Hîsyêô community.

:::

### Citizens

The term "citizen" is a way for state actors to differentiate between people
inside and outside of their imagined communities. You may be asked or need to
ask someone of their citizenship.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>nîmû li kukwon yê kûôcyo Yûnoitit Sitêtsu ko.&emsp;<em>Are you a US citizen?</em></p>
    <p>nîo yûnyoû kon kukwon nîo yê kûôcyo Êlînîkî ko.&emsp;<em>Do they have their Greek citizenship?</em></p>
    <p>noyo yûnyoû dûî kon kukwon yê kûôcyo Kenudu ûn kûôcyo Ustulêilyu.&emsp;<em>I have dual Canadian and Australian citizenship.</em></p>
    <p>nûs lufono golo fos sôlû oxon xe li kukwon yê kûôcyo Ûkluyînu. &emsp;<em>We are applying to be Ukrainian citizens.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
