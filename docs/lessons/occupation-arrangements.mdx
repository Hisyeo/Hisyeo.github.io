# Occupation & Arrangements

Everybody has a job to do and coordinating job duties amongst specialists is one
of the hallmarks of human society. There is one word that means occupation or
profession in Hîsyêô: <HisyeoWord is='wêloi'/>. As a verb, it requires some care
with your sentence structure. 

:::warning Translation challenge for "job" and "work"

Within a profession, one has various job duties or tasks, and these are often
translated with the generic word for doing (<HisyeoWord is='tûu'/>). There is no
exact equivalent word for the English catch-all words "job" and "work".

If you are speaking of specialization and expertise, use <HisyeoWord is='wêloi'/>.

If you are thinking of a task or a group of tasks (whether specific or not), use <HisyeoWord is='tûu'/>.

:::

## Talking about Jobs

The word <HisyeoWord is='wêloi'/> is a verb that means "to work [as]" which
means that object of this transitive verb is the occupation. Therefor, to ask
somebody their profession, just use the interrogative pronoun in place of object.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>¿nîmû wêloi zik?&emsp;<em>What is your occupation?</em>&emsp;<small>(lit: You work as what?)</small></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

When answering this question, you treat the profession description as the object of the verb (often times with a null subject).

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>(noyo) wêloi ônî cingî.&emsp;<em>I am an electrician.</em>&emsp;<small>(lit: I work as an elictricity one.)</small></p>
    <p>(noyo) wêloi ônî cûdo yê cîgîlsu hokôlî.&emsp;<em>I am a dentist.</em>&emsp;<small>(lit: I work as a giver of teeth treatments.)</small></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

If you want to ask where someone works, you can skip the theme and use a locative preposition.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>¿nîmû wêloi hoî zik?&emsp;<em>Where do you work?</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
