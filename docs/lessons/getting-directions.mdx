# Getting Directions

Navigating a new city or finding a specific building requires a mastery of
spatial vocabulary. In Hîsyêô, giving directions relies heavily on the
**Locative Nucleus Construction** (treating locations as possessed parts) and
the **Radial Prepositions** (In/Before vs. On/After).

## Travel vocabulary


| English    | Hîsyêô                       | Literal Meaning                    |
|------------|------------------------------|------------------------------------|

### Asking for Directions

To ask where something is, you use the location preposition <HisyeoWord is='hoî'/> combined
with the question word <HisyeoWord is='zik'/>.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>gubuyo yê côûfon (li gô) hoî zik?&emsp;<em>Where is the restaurant?</em></p>
    <p>noyo kôî yosten tîton mût sinsî bi zik?&emsp;<em>How can I find the way to the city? (Lit: By what manner?)</em></p>
    <p>û niwos nîmû (li gô) hoî zik?&emsp;<em>Where is your house?</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Giving Directions

When giving directions, you often use the imperative form (starting with <HisyeoWord is='kûu'/>).

| English                  | Hîsyêô                                                  | Literal Meaning                   |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Go Straight              | <HisyeoText>kûu li nô mûstokîn</HisyeoText>             | Be straight moving                |
| Turn Left                | <HisyeoText>kûu li infultîn mût xîmol</HisyeoText>      | Rotate towards left               |
| Turn Right               | <HisyeoText>kûu li infultîn mût yûkwoî</HisyeoText>     | Rotate towards right              |
| Stop                     | <HisyeoText>kûu nîltûgu</HisyeoText>                    | Stop                              |
| Cross                    | <HisyeoText>kûu tuluti</HisyeoText>                     |                                   |
| Pass                     | <HisyeoText>kûu tuluti sôbo yê</HisyeoText>             | Traverse the side of...           |
| If you're facing left... | <HisyeoText>hôn fos nîmû bît li bono xîmol</HisyeoText> | Given you would be left facing... |

### Describing Locations

Use the Spatial Relations system to describe where buildings are relative to each other.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>îlê tômôs (li gô) hoî fôhon yê gubuyo.&emsp;<em>The library is in front of the store.</em></p>
    <p>kûu li nô mût sôbo yê niwos cênbô.&emsp;<em>Go to the side of the large residence.</em></p>
    <p>ni (li gô) hoî soden yê comen.&emsp;<em>It is behind the field.</em></p>
    <p>kûu li nô ilik sêdu yê oûtô kîskô.&emsp;<em>Go inside the train station.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Distance

To talk about how far something is, use the distance preposition <HisyeoWord is='lon'/>.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>ni li gô hoî mônbili cûtî.&emsp;<em>It is a short distance away. (Lit: It is at a small distance).</em></p>
    <p>ni li gô hoî mônbili cênbô.&emsp;<em>It is far away. (Lit: It is at a large distance).</em></p>
    <p>nûs kûu li nô lon tîû êfun.&emsp;<em>We should walk for five minutes.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Public Transportation

Navigating the transit system requires knowing the types of vehicles.

| English | Hîsyêô                                       | Literal Meaning     |
|---------|----------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Vehicle | <HisyeoWord is='oûtô'/>                      | to drive a vehicle  |
| Train   | <HisyeoText>oûtô kîskô</HisyeoText>          | Rail car            |
| Bus     | <HisyeoText>oûtô kîldo fîmôsyôs</HisyeoText> | Public long vehicle |
| Station | <HisyeoText>sêdu</HisyeoText>                |                     |
| Ticket  | <HisyeoText>doînbî</HisyeoText>              |                     |

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>û oûtô kîskô ke li gîk hoî wokût zik?&emsp;<em>When will the train arrive?</em></p>
    <p>noyo buswen xe wedo til doînbî.&emsp;<em>I need to buy a ticket.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
