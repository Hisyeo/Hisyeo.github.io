# Spatial Relations

Hîsyêô uses a mix of specific prepositions for radial relationships (In/On) and
a locative noun construction for relative positions (Front/Back/Top/Bottom).

This system is governed by the **Radial Growth Metaphor**: just as a tree grows
outward, Hîsyêô speakers perceive "Inside" as the past/origin and "On/Surface"
as the future/destination.

## Radial Prepositions (Space & Time)

These two prepositions handle the fundamental relationship of containment and sequence. They replace the English concepts of "In" and "On" while simultaneously handling "Before" and "After."

| Preposition | Spatial Meaning | Temporal Meaning       | Metaphor              |
|:------------|:----------------|:-----------------------|:----------------------|
| <HisyeoWord is='ilik'/>    | **In / Inside** | **Before / Preceding** | The Core / The Memory |
| <HisyeoWord is='cihôû'/>   | **On / Upon**   | **After / Succeeding** | The Bark / The Growth |

**Examples in Space:**

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>nîo li gô ilik niwos.&emsp;<em>He is in the house.</em><small>(Contained by it)</small></p>
    <p>tômôs li gô cihôû mîêbil.&emsp;<em>The book is on the table.</em><small>(Resting on the surface)</small></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

**Examples in Time:**

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>ilik lono ôfo...&emsp;<em>Before today...</em><small>(In the time preceding today)</small></p>
    <p>cihôû lono ôfo...&emsp;<em>After today...</em><small>(On top of today / In the future)</small></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

:::info The Vocabulary of Radial Time

Because Hîsyêô views time as **growth** (like tree rings or layers of sediment) rather than a straight line, we avoid linear adjectives like "long" or "short" when talking about duration. Instead, we use words describing **volume**, **thickness**, and **depth**.

| Concept                 | Linear Metaphor (Avoid) | Radial Metaphor (Use) | Hîsyêô Word                                       |
|:------------------------|:------------------------|:----------------------|:--------------------------------------------------|
| **Long Duration**       | "A long time"           | **A thick time**      | <HisyeoWord is='côt'/>                            |
| **Short Duration**      | "A short time"          | **A thin time**       | <HisyeoWord is='cencî'/>                          |
| **Distant Past/Future** | "Far away"              | **Deep**              | <HisyeoWord is='zîlyoîs'/>                        |
| **Near Past/Future**    | "Close"                 | **Shallow**           | <HisyeoWord is='dohîl'/>                          |
| **Timeline**            | "Line"                  | **Ring/Circle**       | <HisyeoWord is='wutun'/> <HisyeoWord is='wokût'/> |

**Examples:**
<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>nûs motsi sî lon wokût côt.&emsp;<em>"We waited for a <strong>thick</strong> (long) time."</em></p>
    <p>ni bi sî gô hoî wokût zîlyoîs.&emsp;<em>"It happened in <strong>deep</strong> (distant) time."</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
:::

## Locative Nucleus Construction

For specific physical orientations that are not strictly "In" or "On," Hîsyêô
uses the generic preposition <HisyeoWord is='hoî'/> (At) combined with a
"Position Word" acting as the nucleus.

### Formula:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>hoî&emsp;<strong>+</strong><span>&emsp;[Position Word]&emsp;</span><strong>+</strong>&emsp;yê&emsp;<strong>+</strong><span>&emsp;[Reference Object]</span></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

<em>Literal translation: "At the [top/bottom/inside] of the object."</em>

## Position Words

While these words may belong to other word classes (such as modifiers or verbs),
when placed in this specific syntactic slot, they function as the nucleus of the
phrase to define location.

<HisyeoTabs>

    <p><strong>He stood in front of me.</strong>&emsp;nîo li totsi hoî fôhon yê noyo.&emsp;<em>(He stood at the front of me.)</em></p>
