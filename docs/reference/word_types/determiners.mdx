# Determiners

A type of word that describes the reference type of a noun. You could think of
determiners as a special class of noun modifiers and the only ones that exist
before the head noun.

> [determiners] [head noun] [modifiers]

Determiners involve something related to plurality (quantity), adequacy, or
time. They are the fundamental attributes of nouns alongside what makes each
noun unique.

## Quantity

You can provide numerals as determiners and they allow you to specify the
plurality or quantity of the nouns being referred to. Larger numbers are created
by using the below method of assembling parts of a number:

Add one of these before your noun and you have provided a quantity determiner (a
cardinal number).

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p>son sütüö lı cok sün zomo.&emsp;<em>"Three friends are leaving the group."</em></p>
  <p>nü puswen son dus tiü ëvun oxon xe piek nı süwit övo.&emsp;<em>"We need 35 minutes to bake this cake."</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Providing a quantity determiner of "one" (<HisyeoWord is='kut'/>) can be used as a kind of
indefinite article. Like the English article "a" or "an".

### Ordinal

Place a numeral word as a regular modifier (after the head noun) and it will
represent a relative position of an item in a sequence (an ordinal number).

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p>lı nomü son xe sün ximol, ü süko noyo.&emsp;<em>"The third tree from the left is my favorite."</em></p>
  <p>noyo lı umo nonkü xe lı cok.&emsp;<em>"I was the fourth person to leave."</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Multiplier

Place a numeral word after (<HisyeoWord is='öni'/>) and use this as a modifier to
represent the number of times something is to be multiplied.

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p>poholon yünyoü zınkon öni düi.&emsp;<em>"The closet has double doors."</em></p>
  <p>niün lı kiösö ëto köi tüu zoston öni nonkü pı opunyun.&emsp;<em>"He's quite athletic and can do quadruple jumps with ease."</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Adverbial

Use the same form as above but with the manner marker to get adverbial numerals.

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p>noyo pı öni kut tölki mokon si piek potuk yë öni mulö&emsp;<em>"I have only once eaten pizza"</em></p>
  <p>nimü pı öni düi küu yön sikot hoköli hön lono xokön.&emsp;<em>You should use your toothbrush twice a day.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Distributive

You can make distributive numerals using <HisyeoWord is='xokön'/>. First, to
indicate that a predicate is occurring multiple times with the specified
details, you would tack on a <HisyeoWord is='xokön'/> at the end of a noun
phrase. Here's a non-distributive action and contrasting distributive one:

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p>umo Tom ü umo Zıl oköt son dıpo.&emsp;<em>"Tom and Jill carried three boxes."</em></p>
  <p>umo Tom ü umo Zıl oköt son dıpo xokön.&emsp;<em>"Tom and Jill carried three boxes each."</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

In order to specify that something is double the degree of the associated
noun-phrase. You prefix a quantity phrase with <HisyeoWord is='xokön'/>.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>motüo wohi noyo pı xokön düi diyondë hön xe pı elentët lı ëo ınvultin öni düstu woküt.&emsp;<em>My mother was always doubly careful when winding the clock.</em></p>
    <p>lı sonkon ü xe pesol zınkon, ün pı xokön son hön pıtün.&emsp;<em>It is important to lock the door, and triply so at night.</em></p>
    <p>lolü nimü oncëmon ëo xokön düi tönkö hön doho noyo.&emsp;<em>Your words feel doubly hurtful given my sacrifices.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Relative

Relative determiners provide relativistic information about the head phrase that
they are attached to. They can be relative quantities which are like the above
numerical quantities but with a non-exact non-finite understanding of what the
exact numerical quantity the noun actually is. 

<HisyeoTabs>
  <p><strong>total/all:</strong>&emsp;möi sütüö noyo süko kontol doi osyen.&emsp;<em>All my friends like classical music</em></p>
  <p><strong>highest/most:</strong>&emsp;övo lı numüno yë eswen sonkon en möi.&emsp;<em>This is the most important example.</em></p>
  <p><strong>high/many:</strong>&emsp;lı ëto toliko pësu yë xe cöüvon.&emsp;<em>"There are many different ways to cook a meal."</em></p>
  <p><strong>higher/more:</strong>&emsp;lı eswen toliko yë xe tüu övo, en vos noyo köi tüek vio kut.&emsp;<em>"There are more ways to xe this than I can count."</em></p>
  <p><strong>lower/fewer:</strong>&emsp;lı mënüs umo hoi övo pı ipüdü.&emsp;<em>"There are less people here now."</em></p>
  <p><strong>low/some:</strong>&emsp;yoüti umo süko kon mühoyon.&emsp;<em>"Some people like camping."</em></p>
  <p><strong>lowest/least:</strong>&emsp;noyo pı tölki yünyoü zötëstö oxon xe cıvü mënüs cıno yë nıwos en möi.&emsp;<em>I can only afford to pay the least of the bills.</em></p>
  <p><strong>zero/none:</strong>&emsp;xünyu yë düi umo lı sumon.&emsp;<em>No two people are the same.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
