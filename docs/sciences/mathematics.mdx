# Mathematics

Mathematics, or <HisyeoWord is='sôhoûk'/>, is considered the language of the First Law—the underlying logic of the universe before the Shattering. It is the study of quantity, structure, and change.

## Basic Vocabulary

| English     | Hîsyêô       | Literal Meaning                        |
|-------------|--------------|----------------------------------------|
| Number      | <HisyeoWord is='mîkdol'/>       | Amount / Number                        |
| Zero        | <HisyeoWord is='xûnyu'/>        | Void / Nothing                         |
| Infinity    | <HisyeoWord is='sônsûs'/>       | Endless / Infinite                     |
| Equation    | <HisyeoWord is='wofok'/> <HisyeoWord is='sôhoûk'/> | Arithmetic proof                       |
| Shape       | <HisyeoWord is='xîncûon'/>      | Form / Shape                           |
| Measurement | <HisyeoWord is='dûstu'/>        | To measure (Verb) / Measurement (Noun) |

## Operations

Operations are often expressed using prepositions or specific verbs.

| Operation        | Hîsyêô       | Example (2 + 2 = 4)                                                        |
|------------------|--------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Plus (Add)       | <HisyeoWord is='ûn'/> (and)     | <HisyeoText>dûî ûn dûî li nonkû.</HisyeoText>                              |
| Minus (Subtract) | <HisyeoWord is='sûn'/> (from)   | <HisyeoText>dûî sûn nonkû li dûî.</HisyeoText> (Two from four is two).     |
| Times (Multiply) | <HisyeoWord is='xokôn'/> (each) | <HisyeoText>dûî xokôn dûî li nonkû.</HisyeoText> (Two each two is four).   |
| Divide           | <HisyeoWord is='fun'/> (part)   | <HisyeoText>dûî fun nonkû li dûî.</HisyeoText> (Two parts of four is two). |
| Equals           | <HisyeoWord is='li'/> (is)      |                                                                            |

## Algebra

Algebra involves the study of symbols and the rules for manipulating these symbols.

| English  | Hîsyêô             | Literal Meaning           |
|----------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| Variable | <HisyeoText>mîkdol (dês fisun)</HisyeoText> | Unknown number            |
| Constant | <HisyeoText>mîkdol totsi</HisyeoText>       | Standing number           |
| Function | <HisyeoText>ondo sôhoûk</HisyeoText>        | Arithmetic preparation    |
| Graph    | <HisyeoText>toswîl sôhoûk</HisyeoText>      | Arithmetic drawing        |
| To Solve | <HisyeoText>yosten</HisyeoText>             | To discover / To find out |

Example Problem:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>kûu yosten mîkdol dês fisun Xo.&emsp;<em>Solve for X.</em></p>
    <p>dûî xokôn mîkdol Xo li dus. (zuwobû: tîû)&emsp;<em>2 times X equals 10. (Answer: 5)</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Geometry

Geometry (<HisyeoWord is='xîncûon'/>) deals with the properties of space and the forms that occupy it.

### Fundamental Elements

| English     | Hîsyêô                      | Description                             |
|-------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Point / Dot | <HisyeoWord is='bindû'/>    | A specific location in space.           |
| Line        | <HisyeoWord is='gîto'/>     | A connection between points.            |
| Edge / Rim  | <HisyeoWord is='dînzoû'/>   | The boundary of a shape.                |
| Straight    | <HisyeoWord is='mûstokîn'/> | Lacking curvature.                      |
| Curved      | <HisyeoWord is='wonkûk'/>   | Bending or deviating from straightness. |

### Shapes & Solids

| English       | <HisyeoWord is='Hîsyêô'/>  | Description                                              |
|---------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Triangle      | <HisyeoWord is='mûkônun'/> | A three-sided polygon.                                   |
| Circle / Ring | <HisyeoWord is='wutun'/>   | A continuous curved line equidistant from a center.      |
| Sphere        | <HisyeoWord is='gundolê'/> | A perfectly round 3D object.                             |
| Block         | <HisyeoWord is='dîncî'/>   | A solid object with flat faces (cube/rectangular prism). |
| Tube          | <HisyeoWord is='môkôfo'/>  | A hollow cylinder.                                       |
| Cone          | <HisyeoWord is='tohilôk'/> | A solid tapering to a point from a circular base.        |
| Pyramid       | <HisyeoWord is='cokwoli'/> | A structure with triangular outer surfaces.              |

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>mûkônun fuî niswî sôbo.&emsp;<em>A triangle has three sides.</em></p>
    <p>li xôn eswen û gîto mûstokîn en gîto wonkûk.&emsp;<em>A straight line is better than a curved line.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Describing Polygons

For shapes that do not have a specific name, you can construct a descriptive name using xîncûon (shape) followed by the number of sôbo (sides).

Formula:&emsp;<HisyeoWord is='xîncûon'/>&emsp;<HisyeoWord is='yê'/>&emsp;+&emsp;[Number]&emsp;+&emsp;<HisyeoWord is='sôbo'/>

| English  | Hîsyêô         | Literal Meaning  |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| Pentagon | <HisyeoText>xîncûon yê tîû sôbo</HisyeoText>    | Shape of five sides  |
| Hexagon  | <HisyeoText>xîncûon yê toû sôbo</HisyeoText>    | Shape of six sides   |
| Octagon  | <HisyeoText>xîncûon yê tukwos sôbo</HisyeoText> | Shape of eight sides |
| Polygon  | <HisyeoText>xîncûon yê êto sôbo</HisyeoText>    | Shape of many sides |

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>xîncûon yê toû sôbo fuî toû sôbo.&emsp;<em>A hexagon has six sides.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
