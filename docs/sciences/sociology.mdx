# Sociology

Sociology is the study of zomî (society) and zomo (groups). In Hîsyêô, sociology
is not just an academic observation but a tool for constructing the Great
Weave—a society based on voluntary association rather than coercion.

## Core Concepts

| English     | Hîsyêô                                 | Literal Meaning               |
|-------------|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Society     | <HisyeoWord is='zomî'/>                | Society / Civilization        |
| Group       | <HisyeoWord is='zomo'/>                | Group / Team                  |
| Community   | <HisyeoText>zomo în niwos</HisyeoText> | Residential group             |
| Culture     | <HisyeoWord is='kûngîyo'/>             | Traditionalness/Customariness |
| Norm        | <HisyeoText>fûtsûn zomî</HisyeoText>   | Social rules                  |

## Social Dynamics

How do groups interact and change?

**Solidarity:** <HisyeoText>kotilmok yoldûn yê umo dîzel&emsp;<small>(Aid contributing of other people)</small></HisyeoText>

**Conflict:** <HisyeoWord is='gûexo'/>

**Cooperation:** <HisyeoText>kotilmok xe til xokôn dîzel&emsp;<small>(Working each other/together)</small></HisyeoText>

**Assimilation:** <HisyeoText>kon gomî yê dondun&emsp;<small>(Taking of culture)</small></HisyeoText> 

Often viewed negatively as erasing multiplicity.

**Integration:** <HisyeoText>kon ûnîdû yê cizî losol&emsp;<small>(Joining of original self)</small></HisyeoText> 

Viewed positively as adding threads to the Weave.
