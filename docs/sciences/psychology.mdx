# Psychology

Psychology (<HisyeoWord is='bêûdo'/> <HisyeoWord is='dûuncît'/>) is the study of
the <HisyeoWord is='dûuncît'/> (mind), <HisyeoWord is='kon'/> <HisyeoWord is='gonîso'/> (thinking),
<HisyeoWord is='kon'/> <HisyeoWord is='oncêmon'/> (feeling), and <HisyeoWord is='zusbot'/> (emotions).
In Hîsyêô, mental health is viewed through the lens of the [Shattering](../religion/shattering)—
understanding how the mind fragments under trauma and how it can be re-woven.

## Core Concepts

The Hîsyêô model of the psyche distinguishes between the rational mind, the
emotional heart, and the fleeting thought.

| English       | Hîsyêô                                  | Literal Meaning               |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Mind          | <HisyeoWord is='dûuncît'/>              |                               |
| Reason        | <HisyeoWord is='môtîfô'/>               |                               |
| Emotion       | <HisyeoWord is='zusbot'/>               |                               |
| Thought       | <HisyeoWord is='gonîso'/>               | Idea / Impulse                |
| Feeling       | <HisyeoWord is='oncêmon'/>              | Sensation / Feeling           |
| Consciousness | <HisyeoWord is='kôxentê'/>              |                               |
| Personality   | <HisyeoText>zomo yê dî umo</HisyeoText> | Collection of personal traits |

## Mental Health

Mental illness (<HisyeoWord is='sobûnî'/> <HisyeoWord is='dûuncît'/>) is not
seen as a defect, but as a reaction to the Shards (structures of domination) or
the Reflected Suffering (lingering trauma) of the world.

**Trauma:** <HisyeoText>gûlû zusbot&nbsp;<small>(Emotional wound)</small></HisyeoText>

**Anxiety:** <HisyeoText>cînzon cênbô&nbsp;<small>(Big worry)</small></HisyeoText>

**Depression:** <HisyeoText>kûyô yinsûk yê wokût côt&nbsp;<small>(Serious sadness of a long time)</small></HisyeoText>

**Psychiatry:** <HisyeoText>noûku cîgîlsu dûuncît&nbsp;<small>(Mental treatment science)</small></HisyeoText>

## Reflected Suffering aka "Echoes"

<HisyeoWord is='tônkô'/> <HisyeoWord is='tîkêl'/> (literally "reflected
suffering") is the Hîsyêô term for PTSD or recurring trauma responses. It is the
sound of the Shattering still vibrating in the mind, reflecting off the walls of
memory.

Conversely, the act of processing this trauma—looking at it in the mirror—is
called <HisyeoWord is='kon'/> <HisyeoWord is='tîkêl'/> <HisyeoWord is='tônkô'/> (Pain Reflection).

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo tenkîn tônkô tîkêl yê gûexo.&emsp;<em>I hear the reflected suffering of the war.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

## Therapy & Practice

Psychology in Hîsyêô is a practical discipline of môtsilê (weaving) and unbinding.

**Therapy:** <HisyeoText>cîgîlsu kon kôto&nbsp;<small>(Talking treatment)</small></HisyeoText>

**Meditation:** <HisyeoText>tûu côt yê dûuncît homûs&nbsp;<small>(Long action of a quiet mind)</small></HisyeoText>

**Reflection:** <HisyeoText>kon tîkêl dûuncît&nbsp;<small>(Mental reflection)</small></HisyeoText>

## The Mirror Ritual

A common practice for self-integration, associated with [Xôcibîlî](../religion/gods/xôcibîlî).

1. <HisyeoWord is='dêko'/>: Look at yourself (Observe).

2. <HisyeoWord is='kôto'/>: Speak the truth (Admission).

3. <HisyeoWord is='wofok'/>: Accept the self (Agreement).
