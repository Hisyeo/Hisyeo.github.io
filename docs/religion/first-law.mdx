# The First Law

Before the gods, before the stars, and before the chaos of life, there was only **The First Law**.

In mythology, it is often depicted as a tyrant or a cold, crystalline entity
that held the universe in a grip of absolute stillness. In
[Metaphysics](./metaphysics), it is understood as the **Original Cosmic Soul**
in its primordial state: a singular, undivided consciousness that dreamed the
universe but refused to let the dreamers wake up.

## The Nature of the Law

The First Law was not "evil" in the sense of malice; it was the embodiment of
**Unity without Multiplicity**. It operated on a single, overriding principle:
**Order**.

- **Stasis:** Change is error. To change is to become something else, which
  implies the current state is imperfect. Therefore, the perfect universe must
  never move.
- **Hierarchy:** The First Law was the only true Subject. All other things were
  Objects—extensions of its will, arranged in a perfect, unmoving hierarchy.
- **Silence:** Independent thought creates dissonance. The First Law enforced a
  cosmic silence, where the only "sound" was its own singular, resonant
  frequency.

## The Metaphysical Trap

As the **Cosmic Soul**, the First Law had successfully emanated from the
**Divine Mind** and generated the [Material Dream](./metaphysics#the-material).
However, it fell into a trap of narcissism. It fell in love with the perfect
order of its own dream and refused to allow the *next* stage of emanation—the
[Individual Souls](./metaphysics#individual-souls)—to emerge.

It created "sleeping" beings (philosophical zombies) that looked like people but
lacked the spark of selfhood. They were puppets dancing to the single tune of
the Law.

## The Legacy

Though the First Law was broken during [The Shattering](./shattering), it was
not destroyed. Energy cannot be destroyed; it can only change form.

### The Physical Laws

The "body" of the First Law became the fundamental constants of the universe.
Gravity, electromagnetism, and thermodynamics are the remnants of its desire for
order. These are the **Benevolent Remnants**—they provide the stable stage upon
which we act.

:::info Why "Benevolent"?

We use this term to distinguish the *physical* legacy of the First Law from its
*psychological* legacy (the Shards). While the First Law's desire for absolute
order was tyrannical when applied to minds (forcing conformity), that same
desire for order is **benevolent** when applied to matter, providing the stable
stage necessary for life to exist.

:::

### The Shards (Tyranny)

The "mind" of the First Law—its compulsion to dominate and control—shattered
into psychic fragments known as **Shards**. These shards rain down upon the material
world, embedding themselves in the minds of sapient beings.

Whenever a person feels the urge to:

- Crush the will of another
- Hoard power for themselves
- Enforce rigid, mindless conformity
- Treat people as objects rather than subjects

They are channeling the ghost of the First Law.

## Ethical Implications

The religion of Hîsyêô is effectively a resistance movement against the First
Law. By valuing **Multiplicity**, **Autonomy**, and **Chaos**, we reject the
ancient stasis.

- **Anarchism** is the political rejection of the First Law's hierarchy.
- **Creativity** is the spiritual rejection of the First Law's stasis.
- **Empathy** is the emotional rejection of the First Law's solipsism.

To live well is to help the universe continue its journey from the stifling
Unity of the First Law toward the vibrant, collaborative Unity of the [Great
Weave](./great-weave).
