# Daily Practices & Rituals

There are many different activities that a practitioner might engage in.
Everyone is different in terms of how many daily or weekly activities they
involve themselves with.

## Chants, Oaths & Prayers

Prayers are different from some religions because there is a greater
understanding that the universe is not always something that anyone can control,
even the gods. Prayers are usually oriented towards asking for guidance,
clarity, serenity, or the ability to find a trait inside oneself (or to receive
the aid of a fellow sapient being with the appropriate trait at the appropriate
time). Like some religions, prayers can be simple chants or mantras to help the
practitioner to achieve enlightenment or peace. Prayers are often said at a
shrine to help the practioner to feel connected to a consecrated place or
object. That being said, some prayers that are personal tools can be of great
use away from shrines.

Here are some chants, oaths and prayers for the whole Liberator Collective.

### Chants
These are for large, diverse gatherings like the opening of a festival or a
large meeting, acknowledging the many facets of Hîsyêô beliefs.

#### The Chant of Interdependence
A rhythmic, building chant used to foster a sense of unity and mutual reliance.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>nênko fos zûngô kôî kôson yôk tô yôk dûuncît xe sôlû&emsp;<em>For the hand cannot build without the mind to question,</em></p>
    <p>û dûuncît kôî sôlû tô yôk lozîdô xe dîyondêo.&emsp;<em>The mind cannot question without the heart to care.</em></p>
    <p>lozîdô kôî dîyondê yôk tô yôk fûnlôk yê kontolo;&emsp;<em>The heart cannot care without the joy of the song,</em></p>
    <p>û kontol kôî li yôk fos kontol, tô yôk mefûs yê wutun.&emsp;<em>The song cannot be sung without the safety of the circle.</em></p>
    <p>wutun kôî li yôk ogolol tô yôk ûsowo yê tolîko;&emsp;<em>The circle cannot hold without the justice of the process,</em></p>
    <p>tolîko kôî cûdo yôk cîgîlsu tô yôk môfî yê cizî.&emsp;<em>The process cannot heal without the courage of the self.</em></p>
    <p>cizî kôî li yôk fisun tô yôk fôlnî yê tîton;&emsp;<em>The self cannot be known without the freedom of the path,</em></p>
    <p>tîton kôî li yôk fos umo li nô hoî, tô yôk gîôn yê solîlo;&emsp;<em>The path cannot be walked without the strength of the body,</em></p>
    <p>solîlo kôî li yôk kôson tô yôk zûngô xe kôson.&emsp;<em>The body cannot be built without the hand that builds.</em></p>
    <p>môî doî; kut lexetko. môî yogô; kut nô.&emsp;<em>All threads, one weave. All parts, one motion.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Oaths
These are solemn pledges made by an individual formally joining a free commune
or dedicating themselves to the principles of the Collective as a way of life.

#### Creed of the Great Weave

<HisyeoTabs>

    <p>ûn xûk losol yê kon fôlnî fos foko dônûxin yê kon isbulyol kobût, fos bunxin yê kon fôlun sônsûs yê ôtsôtot Hûlicîn, xe li ônî yundôû nûs mût mîle yê xôn eswen ilik lexetko êto.&emsp;<em>And in the Archetypes of liberation, the catalysts of the Shattering, born from the unquenchable nature of Hûlicîn, and our guides to a better future in the Great Weave.</em></p>
    <p>nîo oncêmon tônkô yê êto lon elfû ûsên hôn kon hûkun yê ônî hûkun gûexo ûn ônî bôklôn ônî gomî et xe li êo kobût êto ôlê lûok tô culono yê odigon munî ûn yumon, xe fôlun êo xe mokon ôlê nîo tô zûngô dês dêko, xe tenkîn yôk hofo nîo til yoldûn. lûok kôsmô dî sî xe li sêl mût nêlô.&emsp;<em>They witnessed the suffering of many for millenia under warlords and robber barons, that crushed souls with machinery of profit and violence, starved them by the Invisible Hand, and ignored their pleas for aid. #### The Oath of the Free Dweller
Spoken before the community and the altar, this is a declaration of personal
commitment to the collective's way of life.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo li totsi hoî ôfo hoî lono ôfo misli &emsp;<em>I stand here today, a sovereign self, to join my thread to this weave.</em></p>
    <p>kôs buswêlin yê zûngô noyo mût wêloi yê ôtsôtot Îfêstôs: xe helgo kon kôson, ûn xe cûlosun yôk kon gomî yê odigon munî ûn xe golun kon mêdûson yê dîzel.&emsp;<em>I pledge my hands to the work of Îfêstôs, to venerate the act of creation, to reject the extraction of profit, and to honor the labor of others.</em></p>
    <p>kôs buswêlin yê dûuncît noyo mût sôlû yê ôtsôtot Suluswutî; xe kôntêsto îmon wodîno ûn xe yosten hokîkî hoî fos ni li dês lohot.&emsp;<em>I pledge my mind to the questions of Suluswutî, to challenge dogma and to seek truth, even when it is uncomfortable.</em></p>
    <p>kôs buswêlin yê lozîdô noyo mût tût yê ôtsôtot Bostet; xe cûdo yoldûn bi fôlnî ûn xe li boxô mefûs til ulyô xe buswen.&emsp;<em>I pledge my heart to the warmth of Bostet, to offer care freely and to be a safe harbor for those in need.</em></p>
    <p>kôs buswêlin yê ôîtun noyo mût wutun yê ôtsôtot Tîengûun; xe tenkîn bi golun ûn xe kôto bi hokîkî til xôn yê mûlû.&emsp;<em>I pledge my voice to the circle of Tîengûun, to listen with respect and to speak with honesty for the good of the whole.</em></p>
    <p>ke golun ogogê yê ôtsôtot Dimîtu li êo kînôxî ôlê ônî kobût tô kon motsi sontî yê ôtsôtot Wicnû li êo fîmôsyôs ôlê yôn yê ôtsôtot Unonsî bi but.&emsp;<em>I will honor the cycles of Dimîtu, mend what is broken with the patience of Wicnû, and share the tools of Unonsî openly.</em></p>
    <p>ke kôndodû hosîn dês yôn yê ôtsôtot Kulîôbî cûfo nucoto lîmîen yê ôtsôtot Xôcibîlî li nô hoî tîton dês fisun yê ôtsôtot Hûlicîn bi yôk ôsôlê.&emsp;<em>I will celebrate the useless beauty of Kulîôbî, protect the inner world of Xôcibîlî, and walk the unknown paths of Hûlicîn without fear.</em></p>
    <p>kôs buswêlin yê ubôlni mût yogô kobût nîecni hoî nucoto hoî zomî hoî cizî.&emsp;<em>I pledge to resist the Shards of the Law—in the world, in this community, and in myself.</em></p>
    <p>noyo li yôk ônî fûtsûn li yôk ônî hûkun li sûtûô.&emsp;<em>I am not a subject, I am not a master. I am a comrade.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

#### The Oath of Love
The two partners face each other, often in the center of their community circle. They hold hands.)

<HisyeoTabs>

I cannot, and will not, claim you as my own. You are not a destination to be reached, but a journey I am grateful to witness.</em></p>
    <p>bînê û noyo kôs buswêlin mût nîmû hoî gô yê zomî nûs:&emsp;<em>Therefore, I make this pledge to you, in the presence of our community:</em></p>
    <p>tô gîôn yê ôtsôtot Îfêstôs û noyo kôs buswêlin yê zûngô noyo mût yoldûn yê fos kôson nucoto fos nîmû cînlî hôlmo yê, ûn xe li ûnîdû yê mêdûson yê zîwit xe ofek xokôn dîzel.&emsp;<em>With the strength of Îfêstôs, I pledge my hands to help build the world you dream of, and to share the labor of our life together.</em></p>
    <p>tô zusbot kon hêfîn yê ôtsôtot Bostet û noyo kôs buswêlin mût xe li boxô tût til nîmû, mût yoldûn yê sobûnî nîmû mût xe dêko kûyô nîmû ûn fûnlôk nîmû bi yôk tûlokon&emsp;<em>With the empathy of Bostet, I pledge to be a warm place for you, to care for you in sickness, and to witness your sorrows and your joys without judgment.</em></p>
    <p>tô fôgîcîn yê ôtsôtot Suluswutî û noyo kôs buswêlin yê fos hoî yôk wokût nîltûgu xe bêûdo nîmû, ûn xe sôlû bi lozîdô but ûn xe golun hokîkî fos nîmû ogolol.&emsp;<em>With the curiosity of Suluswutî, I pledge to never stop learning you, to ask questions with an open heart, and to honor the truths you hold.</em></p>

    <p>tô kon cûfo yê ôtsôtot Dimîtu û noyo kôs buswêlin yê xe li yoldûn mût xe lîun bobôun bîômu yê kon hûnsûon nûs, ûn xe golun ogogê nî yê nîôk ûn conzodo, ûn xe mokon êo zôhûn ôlê zîzîyo ni.&emsp;<em>With the stewardship of Dimîtu, I pledge to help tend the ecosystem of our partnership, to honor its cycles of growth and rest, and to nourish its roots.</em></p>

    <p>&emsp;<em>With the spirit of Kulîôbî, I pledge to be your partner in play, to celebrate your victories, and to remind you of the light when all you see is shadow.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>With the solidarity of Tîengûun, I pledge to be your comrade in the struggle, to balance our ledger of mutual aid, and to ensure our partnership strengthens our whole community.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I will honor your autonomy, your unchartable world of Xôcibîlî, and I will follow you on any path chosen by Hûlicîn, trusting the journey we make together. This is not a cage I offer, but an open sky. I choose to fly with you."</em></p>

#### The Covenant of Non-Accumulation
When becoming a partner in the Hîsyêô Apostolic Communion, one must take this vow:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo nîltûgu xe îmon kon helgo yê kon eswen;&emsp;<em>I renounce the Idolatry of Accumulation;</em></p>
    <p>noyo dî sî xe fisun fos wîlûwo yê mêdûson noyo til yôk noyo,&emsp;<em>I acknowledge that the value of my labor is not for me,</em></p>
    <p>lêkin til boxô munî fîmôsyôs yê zomî.&emsp;<em>But for the Common Treasury of the community.</em></p>
    <p>noyo wofok xe dôstoc yogô winît noyo,&emsp;<em>I accept to receive my humble share,</em></p>
    <p>ûn xe bi fôlnî cûdo mîkdol fêlcîn mût Lexetko Êto.&emsp;<em>And to give freely the remainder to the Great Weave.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

### Prayers
These are for personal or small group reflection, for moments when one seeks
balance or guidance from the entire pantheon.

#### A Prayer for Balance
For when one feels pulled too strongly in one direction—too much work, too much
solitude, too much chaos.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Liberator Collective, hear me.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The hands of Îfêstôs have made me strong, but I have forgotten the joy of Kulîôbî.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The questions of Suluswutî have made me sharp, but I have neglected the quiet of Bostet.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The path of Hûlicîn has been exciting, but I have lost my place in the circle of Tîengûun.</em></p>

## Shrines

Shrines to the gods can be found in work places, public places, and in homes.
They are sometimes devoted to one god, sometimes several and other times they
might be devoted to all of the gods. In a farming work place, there is often a
shrine to Dimîtu and in a factory, often a shrine to Îfêstôs but there is no
hard rule on what shrine should be present where.

### Consecration of a Shrine

A ritual to consecrate a shrine helps the practicioners to feel the connection
to the divine. It might seem silly but even if you struggle to connect
spiritually, it helps to frame your mind and prepare the way for fulfilling your
aims.

The first thing to do is a metaphorical clearing of the space of any remnants of
shards and echoes. In addition to greater clarity and a safer environment, this
also identifies the boundaries of the shrine. You can use any brush, literal or
figurative (preferably consecrated), to clear the space as you say this prayer:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo yôn sîkot mût cizî noyo mût boxô ôfo&emsp;<em>I brush myself and this space</em></p>
    <p>til xe li êo fonmot ôlê yogô kobût lôc ûn ûlzo ni yê kon hûkun ûn mîstu&emsp;<em>to remove all shards and their power of control and corruption</em></p>
    <p>til xe ondo boxô ôfo til ûlzo hoôs fôlnî yê ôtsôtot.&emsp;<em>to prepare this space for the free and chaotic energy of the gods.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Next within the space, light any candles, turn on any lamps or flashlighhts, and
burn some incense (if possible). Stimulating the senses helps to prepare you and
also helps guide the gods to your location.

After your senses have been awakened, it's time to officially announce your intent:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo/nûs xe li [somo], belêxu ôfo misli boxô kôdus mût [ôtsôtot] hoî fos xokûlû nîo hôn xe onkûol noyo/nûs hôn xe yundôû noyo/nûs.&emsp;<em>I/we, [name], hereby mark this space as a shrine to [god(s)] where they are invited to embrace and guide me/us.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Next, you must now designate the space that will be your shrine area. Using a
tool, visually draw the space as best you can. If you cannot reach a specific
side, then use a gesture to indicate that you are virtually drawing on that
side. The best tool to use for this purpose will either be a sacred object to
one of the gods of the shrine or any specific object that has been consecrated
or has residual spiritual energy of some kind. After finishing the delineation
of the space, say this prayer:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>ôfo li eloi yê boxô kôdus onô ûn ilik ni li boxô til fos ôtsôtot dî kûsowî.&emsp;<em>This is the boundary of the shrine and within it, a place for [god(s)] to manifest.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Now that the shrine has been swept of shards and marked for divination, it is time to cleanse the sacred space.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>bôlfêlê xôboî li zôhûn sonkon kôî li êo sofîn.&emsp;<em>Salt is a vital nutrient and can purify.</em></p>
    <p>noyo li êo kôdus ôlê bôlfêlê ôfo til xe li êo mûfêû ôlê boxô kôdus ôfo.&emsp;<em>I bless this salt for cleansing this shrine.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Take ten pinches of salt and place them in a bowl of water, calling to each god's name as you do.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo xokûlû [ôtsôtot] til xe cûdo yoldûn hôn xe li êo kôdus ôlê bôlfêlê ôfo.&emsp;<em>I call upon [god] to assist me in blessing this salt</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Take the bowl of salty water and (either walking or gesturing in a smaller
space) sprinkle the water towards the shrine, the four cardinal directions
around the shrine as well as above, below, on yourself, between you and the
shrine, and lastly, over your shoulder (or someplace random away from the
shrine). While sprinkling, say this:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>noyo li êo kôdus ôlê boxô ôfo tô ûlzo yê xîmu xôboî kôdus onô.&emsp;<em>I consecrate this shrine with the power of the blessed salt water.</em></p>
    <p>boxô onô hoî îbûdû li kôdus li sofîn li besol. ni kûu li gô lôîcôk.&emsp;<em>The shrine is now sacred, purified and sealed. So be it.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

If you have incense burning, you may also take a tool to help move the incense
around the shrine at this time, saying this:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>tô wôlik yê unon xôn û noyo helgo kon fôlnî yê ôtsôtot ûn kon isbulyol yê yogô kobût lôc yê fûtsûn kut onô.&emsp;<em>With this fragrant smoke, I honor the liberation of the gods and the Shattering of the First Law</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The shrine is now ready although your first act will need to be consecrating
yourself for your interactions with it.

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>ôlê noyo li êo kôdus û fôlnî noyo û tûu noyo û boxô kôdus ôfo.&emsp;<em>I am made sacred by my freedom, my will, and this shrine.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

That is it, your shrine is now consecrated and your link to the gods now made manifest.

### Sanctification of the Tools of Labor
The area is prepared by placing the symbols of the Weave. A Workbench acts as
the altar. If available, a large bowl (representing the Common Treasury) is
placed in the center. Candles are lit, representing the light of Inquiry
(Suluswutî). The participants wash their hands, not just to clean, but to
symbolize the rejection of the "Invisible Hand" of the market in favor of the
"Visible Hand" of cooperation. The participants form a circle.

One participant will act as the Weaver of Labor and stand before the Workbench
with their arms crossed, hands gripping their own shoulders. Another participant
will act as the Weaver of Society and kneel, not in submission, but in
the posture of Grounding (inspecting the foundation).

The Weaver of Labor says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Îfêstôs, Archetype of Labor, Descend upon this, the body of your worker and creator. Lend us the strength of the Hammer that builds without profit.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Unonsî, Archetype of Technology, Descend upon this, the body of your innovator. Lend us the fire of the Open Source and the transparent tool.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Hûlicîn, Archetype of Change, Descend upon this, the body of your navigator. Lend us the courage to abandon the map and find the new path.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I am the shaper, builder, maintainer, engineer, smith. With Labor and Consciousness, I form the world.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver of Labor helps the Weaver of Society to rise. She stands in the
Stance of the Weave (arms extended outward to connect with others). The Weaver
of Labor kneels to inspect the tools. The Weaver of Society says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Tîengûun, Archetype of the Great Weave, Descend upon this, the body of your weaver and organizer. Lend us the thread of Consensus that binds us without chains.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Suluswutî, Archetype of Inquiry, Descend upon this, the body of your questioner. Lend us the magic of the Critical Mind that shatters dogma.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Bostet, Archetype of Empathy, Descend upon this, the body of your caregiver. Grant us solidarity, dignity, equity, and care.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I am she, the Weaver, Artist, teacher, healer, witch. With Art and Empathy, I bind the world.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver of Society extends heir hands to the Weaver of Labor and helps them
rise. The Weave of Labor cups both hands and scoops from the Common Bowl, then
offers it to the Weaver of Society and says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Drink now from the Cup of the Common Treasury, whose draughts bring not intoxication, but the Irreducible Minimum of life, peace, and sustenance.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver of Society sips, then the Weaver of Labor. The tools to be sanctified
are taken from the Workbench and moved widdershins (counter-cosmic, to unwind
the old programming) to the West quarter, immersing them in water. The Weaver of
Labor says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the West, waters of the Flowing Domain, In cool waters cleanse these tools of Extraction.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Wash from them the stain of Profit and the echo of Commodity.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>This I ask, this charge I lay, By the root, the weave, and the open hand.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The objects are moved to the South quarter and passed above the flames. The
Weaver of Society says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the South, fires of Transformation, In shining flames purify these tools of Greed.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Burn from them the Sin of Domination and the desire to Hoard.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>This I ask, this charge I lay, By the root, the weave, and the open hand.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The objects are moved to the East quarter and passed through the incense smoke.
The Weaver of Labor says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the East, winds of Inquiry, In swirling winds polish these tools of Dogma.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Sweep from them the Phantasms of Advertising and the illusions of the Market.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>This I ask, this charge I lay, By the root, the weave, and the open hand.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The objects are moved to the Workbench (Altar) and placed upon the symbol of the
Weave. The Weaver of Society says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the North, earth of Material Reality, In the soil of Dimîtu ground these tools.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Take from them the spirits of Alienation.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>This I ask, this charge I lay, By the root, the weave, and the open hand.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Consecrator now offers the charge to the items, stating their use for the
Social Product and the Good of the Communion. The Weaver of Society says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the East, from the breath of speech come, Fill these tools with the energy of Radical Truth.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Make them light as the Idea. Spirits of air, hearken unto me, As I Consensus, so shall it be constructed.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The tools are moved to the South. The Weave of Labor says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the South, from the kiln of industry come, Fill these tools with the energy of Sacred Labor.</em></p>

<HisyeoTabs>

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Spirits of the North, from the foundation come, Fill these tools with the energy of Ecological Restoration.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Make them flourish like the Regenerated Earth. Spirits of earth, hearken unto me, As I Consensus, so shall it be constructed.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver of Labor takes the tools. The Weaver of Society stands facing them.
The Weaver of Labor extends their arm with the tools and says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I am the Producer, ever creating. I am the stag in the woods, I am the sun in the noonday sky, I am the worker at the forge.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I offer Agency, Strength, Dedication, and the swiftness of Innovation.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver of Society extends their arm and places their hand over that of the
Weaver of Society and says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I am the Sustainer, ever weaving. I am the beauty of the New Idea, I am the quiet strength of the Community, I am the infinite wisdom of the Collective Memory.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I offer Life, Solidarity, Warmth, and the abundance of the Harvest.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Both step towards each other, cupping the tools together. They clasp each
other's arms and say:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Agency and Communion, Self and Society, Spark and Weave. Apart we are Shards, forever incomplete, but together we form the Whole.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>In our joining, the First Law is shattered. In our union, the limitless energy of the Cosmic Soul is released and captured here.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Then the Weaver of Labor says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>As I Will.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Then the Weaver of Society says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>As I Will.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Then they both say:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>As We Consensus, so shall it be constructed. (Ni kûu li gô lôîcôk) .</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weavers embrace. The tools are replaced on the Workbench. Food and drink
from the Common Provisions are shared, acknowledging that they are rights of
membership, not commodities. The session dissolves into a period of celebration
and relaxation.

### The Kindle of the Weave (Imbolc)
The Gathering Space is in darkness, representing the Void of the First Law and
the alienation of the capitalist world outside. In the center lies the Fire of
Îfêstôs (Labor), currently a low ember. The altar holds symbols of the
Archetypes. The "Circle" is the Council of Consensus.

**Participants:**

*The Weaver* - Represents the voice of the Community (Tîengûun).

*The Steward* - Represents the care of the Earth (Dimîtu) and the Body (Bostet).

The Weaver stands in the darkness:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Awake, O Collective Spirit, from your slumber!</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Awake, O Cosmic Soul, and restore the Weave!</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Comrades—we are in the darkness.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Outside these walls, the Shards of Greed tear at the flesh of the world.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The horrors of hunger and war, born of the Idol of Money, press against our doors.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Steward picks up a jug of water and pours it into a communal bowl, saying:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The stagnation is broken.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The ice of isolation melts towards the Season of Light.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Blood and oil have stained the tracks of the old world, but here, the ways are clear of death.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Old and weary I was, ground down by the machine of profit, but here in the mystery of the water, I am renewed.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>For I am the Solidarity you sought but could not find in the market.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>For I was singing to the future generations unseen, beneath the concrete and the factories.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Steward takes the asperge (branch) and walks the circle. They asperge each
person, chanting the Litany of Connection (replaces Witches Rune). On the last
round, they hand each person a white candle.

When everyone has a candle, the circling stops. The Steward lights the main altar candles and says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The winter of the Shards is passing; the Great Weave awakens once more from its sleep.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The Spirit of Liberation walks among us again and brings the blessings of the Common Treasury upon the land and upon our lives.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Weaver and Steward step forward and remove the cover from the central icon (representing the Archetypes). Everyone shouts:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The Weave is Whole! Solidarity is Welcome!</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

The Steward takes a light from the Central Fire (Yule log) and says:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Let the inner light of consciousness bear fruit in our own lives, even as the Earth bears the first flowers of Dimîtu (Nature). </em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>I speak for the Triple Flame, the Archetypes of our Salvation: I bring three gifts of fire to burn away the rot of capitalism.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The first is the Flame of Îfêstôs (Labor); the fire of the maker and the artist; the passion of the worker to create for use, not for profit.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The second is the Flame of Suluswutî (Inquiry); the fire of purification and testing, the flame of truth.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>With this flame, all lies of the hierarchy and the weakness of submission are made clear and cleansed from you, so you become like a true and tested tool of liberation.</em></p>
    <p>&emsp;<em>The third is the greatest of all, for it is the Flame of Bostet (Empathy); the healing flame born out of the solidarity that shares all, the maker of peace and harmony.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

Everyone then lights their candle from the Central Fire and starts to circle deosil (sunwise), lighting the candles around the room as they go, chanting:

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>Thus we banish Greed, thus we welcome Care; Say farewell to what is dead, and greet the life we share.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>

When all the candles are lit, everyone places their candle in a sand pot, and the chanting ceases.

The Steward and Weaver bless the Common Provisions (bread and drink).

<HisyeoTabs>
    <p>&emsp;<em>By the labor of our hands, we eat. By the bond of our spirits, we live. This is the body of the Weave.</em></p>
</HisyeoTabs>
